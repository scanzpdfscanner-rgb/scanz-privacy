1. What Information Do We Collect?

In short: We do not collect personal information directly. Google AdMob collects limited device information automatically.

Information we do not collect

ScanZ does not collect, store, or transmit any of the following to our servers:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or postal address.
  • The contents of documents you scan or import.
  • GPS or network-based location data.
  • Contacts, calendar entries, call logs, SMS, or microphone data.
  • Web browsing history.
  • Health, financial, or biometric information.
  • Account credentials.

The application does not require you to create an account or sign in.

Information stored locally on your device

  • Scanned images, imported photos, generated PDFs, and thumbnail caches — stored in the application's private storage and, when you choose to export, in your device's Downloads folder via the Android MediaStore API.
  • Document metadata — title, file path, timestamps, page count, file size, and watermark state, stored in a local database (Isar).
  • Application preferences — theme, language, and auto-watermark setting, stored via Android SharedPreferences.
  • Diagnostic log files — a rotating set of files totalling no more than 1.5 MB. These logs are never uploaded automatically. They are only shared if you tap "Share logs" in Settings → Diagnostics.

Information collected automatically by Google AdMob

The application displays advertisements through Google Mobile Ads (AdMob). To deliver and measure advertisements, AdMob automatically collects:

  • Google Advertising ID — a resettable, non-permanent identifier used for advertising.
  • IP address.
  • Network status and basic device information.
  • Advertisement interaction data — impressions and clicks.

Note: AdMob may derive approximate location from your IP address. ScanZ itself does not collect or use location data.

AdMob's data practices are governed by Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

2. Android Permissions and Why We Request Them

In short: We request only the permissions necessary to deliver the application's features. Each permission can be revoked at any time via your device settings.

You may revoke any permission at any time via Settings → Apps → ScanZ → Permissions. Revoking a permission will disable the feature that depends on it.

  • Camera — required to scan physical documents using your device's camera. Used only when you initiate a scan.
  • Photos and Media access — required to import images from your photo library. On Android 13 and above, only image files are accessed. On Android 12 and below, broader read access to device storage is used. Used only when you initiate an import.
  • Storage write access — required to save generated PDF files to your device's Downloads folder when you choose to export. On Android 10 and below, legacy storage write access is used. On Android 11 and above, the standard media storage method is used.
  • Notifications — required to display a local notification when a background operation such as PDF export is complete. No notifications are sent from our servers.
  • Internet — required by Google AdMob to fetch and display advertisements. The application does not use the internet connection for any other purpose.

The application does not request access to your location, microphone, contacts, calendar, SMS messages, call logs, body sensors, or any other sensitive permission.

3. How Do We Process Your Information?

In short: We process locally stored data solely to provide application features. AdMob processes device identifiers to deliver and measure advertisements.

We process locally stored information only to operate the features of the application. This processing occurs entirely on your device. We do not analyse, profile, or use your locally stored data for any purpose beyond operating the application.

AdMob processes the Advertising ID and IP address to deliver relevant advertisements and measure advertisement performance. This is carried out by Google under its own terms and privacy policy.

4. What Legal Bases Do We Rely On to Process Your Information?

In short: We process local data to operate the application. AdMob processes data based on legitimate interests or your consent, depending on your jurisdiction.

If you are located in the EU or UK

We rely on the following legal bases under the GDPR and UK GDPR:

  • Legitimate interests — AdMob processes the Advertising ID and IP address on the basis of legitimate interests in delivering and measuring relevant advertising (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR). For the avoidance of doubt: the application does not display a separate first-launch consent screen for users in the EU or UK. You may exercise your right to object at any time via the in-app Settings → Privacy choices control (which switches AdMob to non-personalised mode for all subsequent ad requests), or via your device's Settings → Privacy → Ads (which resets the Advertising ID).
  • Legal obligations — where processing is necessary to comply with applicable law.

Data transfers from AdMob to Google's servers in the United States are governed by Google's Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission.

Note: AdMob's legal basis for behavioural advertising is an evolving area of EU law and has been challenged by some supervisory authorities. If you believe legitimate interests is not an appropriate basis for processing in your jurisdiction, use the in-app opt-out described above, or contact us at scanZpdfscanner@gmail.com.

If you are located in Brazil

Under the Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD), AdMob processes your data on the basis of your consent (Article 7(I) LGPD), collected through the consent screen displayed on first launch. You may withdraw your consent at any time via Settings → Privacy choices.

If you are located in Indonesia

AdMob transfers data to Google's servers in the United States. You may withdraw your consent at any time via Settings → Privacy choices.

If you are located in Malaysia

Under the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), data processing by AdMob is carried out under the Notice and Choice principle. You were notified through the consent screen displayed on first launch and may change your choice at any time via Settings → Privacy choices.
